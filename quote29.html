"Courage isn’t having the strength to go on; it is going on when you don’t have strength."

- Napoleon Bonaparte -

About the Author

Napoleon Bonaparte (1769–1821) was a French military leader and emperor who rose to power during the French Revolution and became one of history’s greatest strategists. Known for his resilience and ambition, he led France to many victories but also faced great defeats. His leadership and determination continue to inspire many.

Understanding the Quote

Why did he write this?
Napoleon understood the hardships of war and leadership. He believed that true courage was not about physical strength but about the will to keep going even when faced with exhaustion, fear, or failure.

When?
Though the exact moment of this quote is uncertain, it reflects the mindset Napoleon held throughout his military campaigns, where persistence often made the difference between victory and defeat.

What does it mean?
This quote teaches that courage is not about having endless strength but about continuing forward even when you feel weak or discouraged. It emphasizes resilience, mental toughness, and the determination to push through difficulties.

How This Quote Can Inspire You

Life often presents challenges where you feel like giving up. This quote reminds you that true courage is not about being strong all the time but about refusing to stop, even when things seem impossible. Whether in personal struggles, career obstacles, or ambitious goals, persistence is the key to success.
