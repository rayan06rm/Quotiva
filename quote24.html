"Don’t watch the clock; do what it does. Keep going."

- Sam Levenson -

About the Author

Sam Levenson (1911–1980) was an American humorist, writer, and television host known for his wit and inspirational insights. His works often carried motivational messages, encouraging perseverance, optimism, and a strong work ethic. His famous book In One Era and Out the Other reflects his unique storytelling style and wisdom.

Understanding the Quote

Why did he write this?
Levenson aimed to inspire people to stay persistent in their efforts. He wanted to emphasize that time should not be seen as an obstacle but as a constant force that moves forward. Instead of worrying about time passing, he encouraged people to keep working toward their goals.

When?
This quote was popularized in the mid-20th century when Levenson shared motivational insights in his writings and speeches. His words remain relevant today, inspiring individuals to stay focused and determined.

What does it mean?
The quote compares time to a clock—always in motion, never stopping. Instead of fixating on time slipping away, we should adopt the same mindset: keep moving forward.

  • Watching the clock often leads to procrastination or frustration.
  • The key to success is continuous effort and resilience.
  • True progress comes from staying focused on your work rather than being distracted by how much time has passed.

How This Quote Can Inspire You

This quote serves as a powerful reminder that consistency is key. Whether in personal growth, career, or any challenge, the secret to success is to keep going, regardless of obstacles. When things get tough, instead of worrying about time running out, shift your focus to taking action—just like the clock, which never stops moving.
