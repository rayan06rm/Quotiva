"Courage is not having the strength to go on; it is going on when you don’t have the strength."

- Theodore Roosevelt -

About the Author

Theodore Roosevelt (1858–1919) was the 26th president of the United States, known for his progressive policies, environmental conservation efforts, and strong leadership. A war hero, writer, and adventurer, he embodied resilience and determination. Roosevelt’s philosophy emphasized perseverance, self-discipline, and the belief that true strength comes from pushing forward despite adversity. His words continue to inspire those facing challenges to persist with courage and conviction.

Understanding the Quote

Why did he write this?
Theodore Roosevelt believed in the power of resilience and inner strength. This quote reflects his life philosophy — that true courage is not about feeling strong, but about continuing forward even when you feel weak or defeated. He aimed to inspire people to push through their hardships with determination.

When?
This quote aligns with Roosevelt’s experiences, from overcoming childhood illness to leading soldiers in battle and facing political challenges. His life was a testament to perseverance, making these words not just inspirational, but deeply personal.

What does it mean?
The quote teaches that courage is not the absence of fear or exhaustion — it’s the decision to keep going despite them. Strength isn’t just physical or emotional energy; it’s the willpower to rise when you feel you can’t. Roosevelt reminds us that real bravery is found in persistence, not in never feeling weak.

How This Quote Can Inspire You

This quote encourages you to find your inner strength during tough times. It reminds you that when you feel like giving up, continuing forward is what builds true courage. Life’s challenges aren’t won by never falling, but by standing back up, time and time again. By embracing this mindset, you grow stronger, more resilient, and more capable of facing whatever lies ahead.
