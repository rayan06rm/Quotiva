Understanding the Quote

Why did he write this?
Newton used this quote to acknowledge the contributions of past scholars who paved the way for his own discoveries. He recognized that scientific progress is built upon the work of those who came before, rather than being achieved in isolation.

When?
Newton wrote this phrase in a letter to fellow scientist Robert Hooke in 1675. It was his way of emphasizing that his advancements were made possible by learning from the knowledge of earlier thinkers, such as Galileo and Kepler.

What does it mean?
The quote highlights the idea that progress is cumulative. Each new discovery builds upon previous knowledge, allowing humanity to advance further than any individual could alone. It teaches humility and reminds us to appreciate the foundation laid by those before us.

How This Quote Can Inspire You

This quote encourages us to seek knowledge from others, whether from teachers, mentors, or historical figures. It reminds us that growth comes not only from personal effort but also from learning, collaboration, and building upon past wisdom. By recognizing the value of those who came before, we can push the boundaries of our own potential and contribute to future progress.
