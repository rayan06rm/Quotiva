"The greater the difficulty, the more glory in surmounting it."

- Epicurus -

About the Author

Epicurus (341–270 BCE) was an ancient Greek philosopher and the founder of Epicureanism, a philosophy centered on seeking happiness through wisdom, friendship, and a simple life. He believed that true pleasure comes from inner peace and intellectual fulfillment rather than material wealth or indulgence. His teachings influenced many later thinkers in ethics and personal well-being.

Understanding the Quote

Why did he write this?
Epicurus emphasized that challenges and hardships are necessary for personal growth. He believed that overcoming difficulties leads to a deeper sense of achievement and fulfillment.

When?
Though the exact date of this quote is unknown, it aligns with Epicurus’ philosophy on resilience and the pursuit of true happiness. His teachings, written around the 3rd century BCE, continue to inspire people today.

What does it mean?
This quote highlights the idea that true success is measured not by ease but by the obstacles we overcome. The harder the journey, the greater the satisfaction in reaching the goal. Struggles shape character, and victory is sweetest when it comes after adversity.

How This Quote Can Inspire You

This quote encourages perseverance in the face of challenges. When difficulties arise, instead of seeing them as roadblocks, view them as opportunities to grow and prove your strength. The struggles you overcome today will become the achievements you take pride in tomorrow.
