"You are never given a wish without also being given the power to make it come true."

- Richard Bach -

About the Author

Richard Bach (1936–present) is an American writer best known for his philosophical and inspirational works. His most famous book, Jonathan Livingston Seagull, explores themes of self-discovery, freedom, and personal growth. Many of his writings emphasize the power of belief, imagination, and perseverance in achieving one’s dreams.

Understanding the Quote

Why did he write this?
Bach wrote this quote to inspire people to believe in their potential. He wanted to remind us that our dreams are not random or impossible—they come with the ability to achieve them, as long as we have the courage and determination to act.

When?
Richard Bach shared this idea in the late 20th century, a time when self-help and motivational literature gained popularity. His works have continued to influence and encourage readers around the world.

What does it mean?
The quote suggests that every desire or ambition we have is accompanied by the ability to make it a reality. It does not mean success is effortless, but rather that we possess the necessary skills, resilience, and inner strength to achieve what we truly want. The key is to trust in ourselves and take action.

How This Quote Can Inspire You

Life is a journey, not a race. Whether you're learning a new skill, building a career, or overcoming obstacles, progress may sometimes feel slow. But as long as you keep pushing forward, growth and success are inevitable. Stay patient, trust the process, and remember that persistence is the key to achieving greatness.
